WHEREAS section 338.1 of the Municipal Act, 2001, S.O. 2001, Chapter 25, as amended (‚ÄúAct‚Äù), provides that the Minister of Finance may, by regulation, designate municipalities as having the ability to pass a by-law to impose a tax in the municipality on the assessed value, as determined under the Assessment Act, R.S.O. 1990, Chapter A.31, as amended (‚ÄúAssessment Act‚Äù), of vacant units that are classified in the residential property class and that are taxable under that Act for municipal purposes;

AND WHEREAS, at its meeting of March 23, 2022, Council approved the establishment of a program which would include the enactment of a vacant unit tax by-law effective January 1, 2022, subject to the enactment of the applicable regulation by the Minister of Finance as referenced above;

AND WHEREAS, on May 3, 2022, the Minister of Finance enacted Regulation 458/22, thereby designating the City of Ottawa as a municipality that may impose a vacancy tax in accordance with the Act and the applicable regulations;

AND WHEREAS section 338.2(2) of the Act provides that a by-law established by a designated municipality to impose a vacancy tax shall satisfy the criteria contained within that section;

AND WHEREAS section 338.2(3) of the Act further provides that a by-law established by a designated municipality to impose a vacancy tax may establish additional requirements as Council considers appropriate;

THEREFORE the Council of the City of Ottawa enacts as follows:

In this by-law:

‚ÄúArm‚Äôs Length‚Äù means a transaction between two independent parties in which both parties are acting in their self-interest to attain the most beneficial deal;

‚ÄúBusiness Day‚Äù means any day other than a Saturday, Sunday or a holiday;

‚ÄúCottage Rental‚Äù means Cottage Rental as defined in the Short-Term Rental (By-law No. 2021-104), as amended;

"Declaration Period" means the period between January 1st of each year and the prescribed due date for the property status declaration;

‚ÄúDeputy City Treasurer, Revenue‚Äù means the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services of the City of Ottawa, or an authorized representative;

"Hazardous Condition" means a structural component of the Property is defective or damaged, oil, gas or another poisonous or dangerous substance is present in the Property, or any other condition relating to the Property that is hazardous to the health or safety of its occupants;

‚ÄúLate Declaration Notice‚Äù means a notice issued to an Owner who has not submitted a declaration by the required due date;

"Major Renovation" means work that, as determined by the Program Administer, impairs the occupants' ability to reside in the Unit. This work may include work that significantly impairs the use of the kitchen or bathrooms;

‚ÄúNotice of Complaint‚Äù means a notice of complaint submitted by an Owner pursuant to section 7 of this by-law;

"Occupancy Year‚Äù means the calendar year prior to the applicable Tax Year for which the property status must be declared;

‚ÄúOccupier‚Äù means an Owner or a person who occupies a Residential Property with the permission of the Owner but is not a Tenant or subtenant;

‚ÄúOwner‚Äù means the person(s)/corporation listed on the property tax roll as the registered owner of the property or their authorized agent;

‚ÄúPrincipal Residence‚Äù means:

(a) the Residential Property that is owned or rented by a person, alone or with others, where the person is ordinarily resident, makes their home and conducts their daily affairs, including, without limitation, paying bills and receiving documentation related to identification, taxation and insurance purposes, driver‚Äôs licenses, income tax returns, medical plan documentation, vehicle registration and voter registration, or similar information; and

(b) where the person has no other property designated as such within the City of Ottawa or any other jurisdiction;

‚ÄúProgram Administrator‚Äù means the Program Manager ‚Äì Revenue Support, or an authorized staff person acting in such capacity to exercise the powers, duties and functions set out in this by-law;

"Renovation Period" means the maximum period of twelve months immediately following vacancy used for the purpose of conducting Major Renovations;

‚ÄúResidential Property‚Äù or "Property" means property classified in the residential property tax class under the Assessment Act;

‚ÄúResidential Unit‚Äù or ‚ÄúUnit‚Äù means a Residential Property, and each dwelling unit is included within the residential assessment of the property;

‚ÄúSublease Agreement‚Äù means an agreement with a term of at least 30 consecutive days:

(a) under which:

(i) the Tenant of a rental unit transfers the Tenant‚Äôs rights under the Tenancy Agreement to a subtenant for a period shorter than the term of the Tenant‚Äôs Tenancy Agreement, and

(ii) the subtenant agrees to vacate the rental unit at the end of the term of the Sublease Agreement, and

(b) that specifies the date on which the tenancy under the Sublease Agreement ends;

‚ÄúTaxable Assessed Value‚Äù means the current tax years‚Äô assessment value supplied by the Municipal Property Assessment Corporation ‚ÄúMPAC‚Äù;

‚ÄúTax Year‚Äù means the calendar year in which the Vacant Unit Tax is imposed;

‚ÄúTenancy Agreement‚Äù means an agreement with a term of at least 30 consecutive days, whether written or oral, express or implied, between a Landlord and a Tenant respecting possession of a Rental Unit;

"Uninhabitable" means unfit for residential occupancy as determined by the Program Administrator in consultation with the Chief Building Official;

‚ÄúUnpredictable‚Äù means that one could have no prior knowledge of the event concerned;

‚ÄúUnpreventable‚Äù means that one could do nothing reasonably in their power to prevent such an event;

‚ÄúVacant Unit Tax‚Äù means the tax imposed at a rate set out in accordance with this By-law and shall be imposed on all properties considered to be eligible property subject to the tax;

‚ÄúVUT Audit Determination Notice‚Äù means a notice issued to an Owner in respect of one or more eligible vacant Units subject to the Vacancy Unit Tax, which confirms as the result of an audit, the Vacancy Unit Tax will be imposed on the residential assessment and issued on a supplementary tax bill in accordance with this by-law.

  1. A Residential Property is considered an eligible property, and the Owner is required to declare its vacancy status if:
    1. it is assessed as a Residential Property at the time of the assessment roll return provided by MPAC; and
    2. it is categorized by MPAC in one of the property codes set out in Schedule ‚ÄúA‚Äù of this by-law.

Vacant Unit Tax 

  1. The Vacant Unit Tax shall be imposed on every eligible Residential Unit that is determined to be vacant in accordance with this By-law.

Vacant Residential Property or Unit - Conditions of Vacancy 

  1. A Residential Unit is considered to be vacant if:
    1. it was unoccupied for more than the aggregate of 184 days during the previous calendar year, was not the Principal Residence of an Occupier, and was unoccupied for more than the aggregate of 184 days for residential purposes by a Tenant under a Tenancy Agreement, or by a subtenant under a Sublease Agreement; or
    2. it is deemed to be a vacant unit by the City of Ottawa in accordance with this by-law.

Vacant Unit Tax Rate

  1. The rate of the Vacant Unit Tax is set out in Schedule ‚ÄúB‚Äù of this by-law.

Vacant Unit Tax Payment

  1. The amount stated as due and payable on the corresponding tax bill issued pursuant to this by-law is due and payable according to the due dates set established in the City's annual Instalment Due Dates and Interest and Penalty Rates Application to the Collection of Property Taxes by-law, as amended.

Date for Payment Pursuant to a Supplementary Vacancy Tax Bill

  1. The amount stated as due and payable on a supplementary tax bill for the Vacant Unit Tax issued pursuant to this By-law is due and payable on the due date set out in the Supplementary Vacancy Tax Notice.

Penalty and Interest Charges - Failure to Pay Vacant Unit Tax

  1. A Vacant Unit Tax, or any portion thereof, which is due and payable and remains unpaid as per the due date in 2.5 and 2.6 of this by-law, shall be charged interest and penalty in the same manner established in the City's annual Instalment Due Dates and Interest and Penalty Rates Application to the Collection of Property Taxes by-law, as amended.

Penalty for Failure to Pay Pursuant to a Supplementary Vacancy Tax Notice

  1. The Vacant Unit Tax, together with any interest levied for failure to pay the tax in accordance with this by-law, will be collected as real property taxes and added to the tax roll for the property, pursuant to the applicable provisions of the Act.

  1. A vacant Residential Unit will be exempt from the Vacant Unit Tax for that Tax Year if, during the Occupancy Year:
    1. it was unoccupied for more than 184 days because of the death of the Owner of the Residential Property;
      1. this exemption applies to the Occupancy Year in which the death occurred, and may be claimed again for the Occupancy Year immediately following the year in which the death occurred.
    2. it was unoccupied for more than 184 days to redevelop or safely carry out Major Renovations to the property:
      1. for which the City has issued permits under the Ontario Building Code Act; and
      2. which, in the opinion of the Program Administrator in consultation with the Chief Building Official, are being carried out diligently and without unreasonable delay; or
      3. in which work began immediately following the Unit becoming vacant, and where the Unit returned to being occupied within the Renovation Period. This exemption can only be claimed once per Owner, per Unit.
    3.  it was unoccupied for more than 184 days because all Occupiers or Tenants who were previously occupying the unit or all Tenants or subtenants who were previously occupying the unit as a principal residence are currently residing in a hospital long term or supportive care facility.
    4. the transfer of 100 percent of the legal interest in the property was registered in the Land Title Office to an Arm‚Äôs Length party.
    5. it was unoccupied for more than 184 days solely because a court order, court proceedings or order of a governmental authority prohibits its occupancy, provided that the court proceedings or any conditions or requirements set out in any court order or order of a governmental authority are being diligently pursued without unnecessary delay by the Owner and within any stated timelines and provided that the Owner does not have an order imposed due to neglect of the property.
    6. it was, for a combined period of at least 184 days:
      1. it was unoccupied in order to redevelop or carry out Major Renovations to the Property:  
        1. for which the City has issued permit(s) under the Building Code Act; and
        2.  the renovations are being carried out diligently and without unreasonable delay as determined by the Program Administrator in consultation with the Chief Building Official; and
      2. occupied for residential purposes by an Arm‚Äôs Length Tenant under a Tenancy Agreement, or by an Arm‚Äôs Length subtenant under a Sublease Agreement, for a term of at least 30 consecutive days either:
        1. prior to the issuance of such permits by the City, where the Tenant or subtenant gave notice to end their tenancy, or  
        2. after the redevelopment or renovation work has been completed.
    7. it was used as a Short-Term Cottage Rental as defined in the Short-Term Rental (By-law No. 2021-104), as amended and: 
      1. The Owner has a host rental permit; and
      2. The unit was rented for more than 100 days with proof of revenue; and
      3. The property is located in one of the following rural zones: AG ‚Äì Agricultural, RC ‚Äì Rural Commercial, RR ‚Äì Rural Residential, RU ‚Äì Rural Countryside other than subzones AG4 to AG8, inclusive.
    8. it was a newly built unit which was unoccupied for more than 184 days; and
      1. it was continuously listed for sale and not sold, or continuously listed for lease and not leased; and
      2. the tax year for which the exemption is claimed is the first year in which the unit was added to the tax roll
    9. it was a rural structure; and
      1. it was  located in a rural zone as defined in City of Ottawa Zoning By-law, as amended; 
      2. it was located on a parcel of at least three acres;
      3. it was determined to be Uninhabitable; and
      4. an appeal was filed by the Owner with MPAC to reassess the Property.
    10. it was unoccupied as it was in a Hazardous Condition for more than 184 days,
      1. that resulted from a disaster that was outside of the control of the Owner
      2. the Owner has taken reasonable steps to address the Hazardous Condition in a timely manner as determined by the Program Administrator; and
      3. this exemption is limited to a maximum of two consecutive Occupancy Years.
    11. it was unoccupied as it was used by the Owner, their spouse, dependent, or caregiver to receive or support medical treatment and where the Unit is required to support such treatment. The Owner shall submit to the Program Administrator a document in the prescribed form and signed by a medical practitioner, which confirms that the Property is in close proximity to the location of the medical treatment.

Administrator 

  1. The Program Administrator will administer the collection of the Vacant Unit Tax and enforcement under this by-law. 

Completion of Property Status Declaration

  1. For each real property tax roll, an Owner must complete either online or by other accessible format and return the property status declaration(s) to the City of Ottawa in the form and manner required by the Program Administrator on or before the due date for the declaration period as the same due date for interim tax-bills established in the City's annual Instalment Due Dates and Interest and Penalty Rates Application to the Collection of Property Taxes by-law, as amended.

Review of Completed Property Status Declaration 

  1. Revenue Services will review each completed property status declaration form and determine whether the information provided by an Owner is sufficient to establish the status of a residential property during the Occupancy Year.

Request for Further Information

  1. The Program Administrator may require an Owner to provide information at any time and for a period of up to two years after the applicable Occupancy Year respecting:
    1. the property;
    2. the identity and address of the Registered Owner;
    3. the identity and address of any person occupying the property;
    4. the status of the property; and/or
    5. the nature of the occupancy of the property during the Occupancy Year.

Requirement to Submit Evidence

  1. The Program Administrator may require the Owner to submit evidence to verify a property status declaration and the status of the Residential Unit.
  2. The information or evidence required by the Program Administrator pursuant to this by-law may include but is not limited to:
    1. copies or certified copies of: 
      1. vehicle insurance and MTO registration;
      2. government-issued personal identification, including, without limitation, a driver‚Äôs licence, Ontario Identity Card:
      3. income tax returns and notices of assessment;
      4. Tenancy Agreements;
      5. wills, grants of probate, or grants of administration;
      6. employment contracts, pay statements or records of employment;
      7. verification of residence in long term or supportive care;
      8. insurance certificates for homeowners or Tenants insurance;
      9. separation agreements;
      10. relevant court orders;
      11. Medical Services Plan or ambulance invoice;
      12. Agreement of Purchase and Sale; and
    2. statutory declarations or affidavits regarding the status of the property.

Power to Request Particulars 

  1. The Program Administrator may require the property Owner who has not submitted a completed property status declaration to provide information or submit evidence in accordance with subsections 4.4, 4.5 or 4.6.

Inspection Power

  1. The Program Administrator may inspect a Residential Unit to confirm or determine the status of a Residential Unit and whether the unit is subject to the Vacant Unit Tax. The Program Administrator will first send a letter to the Owner at the address appearing on the real property tax roll, advising of the time and date of the inspection.

Requirement for Annual Declaration

  1. Owners shall submit to the City of Ottawa, in the prescribed form, an annual status declaration in respect of the previous calendar year by the due dates advising the City whether the Residential Unit was:  
    1. a vacant Residential Unit; and
    2. whether it met the criteria of any of the exemptions set out in section 3.1.

Late or Failure to Submit Declarations

  1. Owners who submit a statutory declaration after the prescribed due date, or fail to submit a statutory declaration, will be charged a Late declaration fee outlined in the City's Revenue Services Fees by-law, as amended. 

Deemed Vacancy

  1. The Program Administrator will accept late declarations until April 30 of the year the declaration is due, subject to the administrative fee set out in subsection 5.2. If the Program Administrator does not receive a declaration by April 30, the Residential Unit shall be deemed vacant and will therefore be charged the Vacant Unit Tax on the final property tax bill.

False Declarations

  1. An Owner shall not submit a false property status declaration.
    1. False declarations may be subject to prosecution under section 9 of this by-law.

Providing Information or Evidence

  1. An owner:
    1. shall provide any information or submit any evidence that is required in accordance with this by-law;
    2. shall provide the information or submit the evidence in the form and within the time stipulated by the Program Administrator; and
    3. shall not provide false information or submit false evidence to the City of Ottawa.

Property considered to be Vacant

(1) A Residential Unit will be deemed vacant and subject to the Vacant Unit Tax if the Owner:  

(a) fails to make a status declaration by April 30 of the applicable Tax Year;

(b) submits a false status declaration;   

(c) fails to provide information or fails to submit any evidence requested by the Program Administrator in accordance with this by-law; or

(d) provides false information or submits false evidence to the City.  

Notice of Complaint 

(1) A complaint regarding the decision of the Program Administrator to impose the Vacant Unit Tax (‚ÄúNotice of Complaint‚Äù) may be submitted by an Owner or the Owner‚Äôs authorized agent (the ‚ÄúComplainant‚Äù) who has been charged the Vacant Unit Tax on the final tax bill or received a Supplementary Vacancy Tax Notice charging a Vacant Unit Tax. The complaint shall be submitted to the Program Administrator on one of the following grounds:

(a) an error or omission on the part of the City resulted in the imposition of the Vacant Unit Tax; or

(b) an error or omission on the Owner in completing the property status declaration resulted in the imposition by the Program Administrator of the Vacant Unit Tax.

Complaint Process

(2) A Notice of Complaint may be submitted to the Program Administrator:

(a) as a result of a property that is deemed to be vacant and the Vacant Unit Tax being levied on the final tax bill, on or before September 15 of the year in which the Vacant Unit Tax is due and payable.

(b) In the case of a VUT Audit Determination Notice, within 90 days of the date that the notice is issued.

(3) The Notice of Complaint shall be submitted in the prescribed form to the Program Administrator.

(3.1) A Late Notice of Complaint may be submitted to the Program Administrator:

(a) for the 2022 Occupancy Year, on or before June 30, 2025 in the prescribed form with the accompanying prescribed fee;

(b) for subsequent Occupancy Years, on or before December 31 of the year following the year in which the Vacant Unit Tax is due and payable, in the prescribed form with the accompanying prescribed fee;

(c) in the case of a VUT Audit Determination Notice, within 450 days of the date the notice is issued, in the prescribed form with the accompanying prescribed fee.

Request for Further Information or Evidence

(4) Upon receiving a Notice of Complaint, the Program Administrator may require the Complainant to provide further information to support the complaint. Failure to do so may result in a refusal by the Program Administrator to consider the complaint. Such refusal will be sent by Program Administrator in writing to the Complainant.

Determination 

(5) The Program Administrator will review a Notice of Complaint and will make a determination within 90 days of receipt of a Notice of Complaint or receipt of the additional information as set out in subsection 7.4.

(6) The Program Administrator will advise the Complainant of their determination in writing by mailing a copy of the determination regarding the notice of complaint by mail to the Complainant.

(7) If the Complainant is successful, Revenue Services will rescind, fully or proportionally as required, the fees and charges related to the Vacant Unit Tax or Supplementary Vacancy Tax within 120 days of the date the determination is made and issue any applicable refunds in accordance with the provisions of this by-law.

Review Process

(1) A request for review of the Program Administrator‚Äôs determination under subsection 7.5 may be made by the Owner or the Owner‚Äôs authorized agent (‚ÄúRequestor‚Äù) in accordance with the procedure set out in this section.

(2) The Requestor shall submit their request for review in the prescribed form to the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services, within 60 days of the date a determination is mailed.

(2.1) A late Request for Review may be submitted to the Program Administrator:

(a) for the 2022 occupancy year, on or before June 30, 2025, in the prescribed form with the accompanying prescribed fee;

(b) for subsequent Occupancy Years, on or before December 31 of the year following the year in which the Vacant Unit Tax is due and payable, in the prescribed form with the accompanying prescribed fee;

(c) in the case of a VUT Audit Determination Notice, within 450 days from the date the notice is issued, in the prescribed form with the accompanying prescribed fee.

Request for Further Information or Evidence

(3) Upon receiving a request for review in the prescribed form, the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services, may require the Requestor to provide further information to support the review. Failure to do so may result in a refusal by the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services, to consider the review.

Decision

(4) The Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services, will review the request and make a decision within 90 days of the receipt of the request for review or receipt of the additional information as set out in subsection 8.3.

(5) The Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services, will advise the Requestor of their decision in writing by mailing a copy of the decision by mail to the Requestor.

(6) The decision will be deemed to have been received four Business Days after the date of mailing.

(7) If the Requestor is successful, Revenue Services will rescind the fees and charges related to the Vacant Unit Tax or Supplementary Vacancy Tax within 120 days of the date the determination is made and issue any applicable refunds in accordance with the provisions of this by-law.

(8) Decisions made by the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue Services, under this section are final.

(1) An administrative fee will be charged to an Owner for:

(a) failing to submit a statutory declaration in accordance with this by-law; or

(b) submitting a statutory declaration after the prescribed due date in accordance with this by-law; and

(c) the ‚ÄúLate Declaration Fee‚Äù will be charged as outlined in the City‚Äôs Revenue Services - User Fee By-Law.

(d) the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue may waive the late declaration fee and any associated refund fee for a declaration not submitted by the prescribed due date if:

(i) an error by the City resulted in the Owner's inability to submit the declaration

(ii) the Owner is away from the subject property for the duration of the declaration period as a result of entering long-term care, hospitalization, or incarceration, with proof of same;

(iii) the Owner passed away during the declaration period, with proof of same;

(iv) the Owner was unable to declare due to being away for the duration of the declaration period as a result of a military posting, education or work assignment, with proof of same;

(v) the Owner was unable to declare as a result of a natural or man-made disaster that disrupted services for an extended period of time during the declaration period, with proof of same;

(vi) the Owner was not notified electronically of the declaration requirement and was unable to return to Canada from abroad due to extenuating circumstances for the duration of the declaration period; with proof of same; or

(viii) for any other extenuating circumstance at the sole discretion of the Deputy City Treasurer, Revenue.

(2) A person who violates any provision of this by-law or who permits or allows any other person to violate this by-law is guilty of an offence.

(3) An offence under section 9.2 will be punishable on conviction by a fine of not less than $500.00 and not more than $10,000.00 for each offence.

Audit Reporting

(1) Revenue Services will perform regular audits to ensure compliance with the by-law and provide periodic reports to Council on compliance levels and audit findings.

(2) Revenue Services has the right to perform audits regarding the declarations for the current reference year plus the two most recent previous Occupancy Years.

Audit Documentation

(3) The Program Administrator may conduct an audit to verify that the Residential Property continues to meet the requirements set out in this by-law, the information provided in the declaration is accurate, and the property is, in fact, occupied. For the purposes of this audit, the Program Administrator may request additional documentation under subsection 4.6 of this by-law and may inspect the property under subsection 4.8 of this by-law.

Non-Compliance

(4) If the Program Administrator determines that an Owner of a Residential Property has not complied with an audit conducted under 10.3:

(a) the property Owner shall be provided with a notice of determination; and

(b) the property shall be reviewed with consideration for additional fines and penalties as applicable under this by-law.

Vacancy Determination 

(5) If, as the result of the audit, the Program Administrator has determined that the property is Vacant, the property will receive a VUT Audit Determination Notice and a corresponding Supplementary Tax Bill with the Vacant Unit Tax levy.

(1) Deadlines in relation to 7.2 (a), 7.2 (b) and 8.2 may be extended for the following reasons:

(a) Extenuating Circumstances due to Unpredictable or Unpreventable circumstances that prevented the application(s) from adhering to prescribed deadlines within this by-law. These circumstances are limited to:

(i) extreme sickness resulting in hospitalization, or

(ii) natural or man-made disasters that disrupted services;

(b) An error by the City.

This by-law shall be deemed to have come into force and effect on January 1, 2022.

(a) The rate of the Vacant Unit Tax for the 2024 Occupancy Year shall be 1% of the Taxable Assessed Value of a parcel of vacant Residential Property;

(b) For each consecutive year in which a Unit is vacant, the rate shall increase by 1% per consecutive Occupancy Year up to a maximum of 5% of the Taxable Assessed Value;

(c) For the 2024 Occupancy Year, any consecutive vacancies shall be deemed a second consecutive vacancy and charged the 2% rate; 

(d) The rate will be charged on the assessed value of a vacant eligible Residential Property;

(e) For Properties with multiple Residential Units assessed under one taxable assessed value, the Vacant Unit Tax will be prorated based on the number of vacant Units in the property divided by the total number of Units in the Property.
