The Ottawa Transition Board pursuant to the City of Ottawa Act 1999, and Regulation 540/00 made thereunder enacts as follows:

Section 1 - Short title

This By-law may be cited as the ‚ÄúProcurement By-law‚Äù. (2017-362)

Section 2 - Purpose

  1. The objective of this By-law respecting procurement is to obtain best value when purchasing goods, construction and services for the City while treating all suppliers equitably.
  2. The guiding procurement principle is that purchases be made using a competitive process that is open, transparent and fair to all suppliers.
  3. These objectives and principles are reflected in this By-law.

Section 4 - Application

The procedures prescribed in this by-law shall be followed to make a contract award or to make a recommendation of a contract award to Council.

Section 5 - Responsibilities and authorities

  1. Directors have responsibility for procurement activities within their service areas and branches and are accountable for achieving the specific objectives of the procurement project. (2017-362)
  2. Directors have the authority to award contracts in the circumstances specified in this by-law provided that the delegated power is exercised within the limits prescribed in this by-law, and the requirements of this by-law are met.
  3. Directors in conjunction with Supply Services shall provide evidence that the contract pricing represents fair market value.
  4. Supply Services is responsible for,
    1. providing professional procurement advice and services to Directors,
    2. monitoring compliance with this by-law,
    3. notifying Directors, in advance if possible, of non-compliance, and
    4. informing Council that non-compliance with this by-law has occurred.
  5. Supply Services has the authority to reject the proposed action of a Director for those actions specified in this by-law requiring a joint ‚ÄúDirector and Supply Services‚Äù decision.
  6. Provided that Supply Services is in receipt of a funded requisition and the requirements of this By-law are met, Supply Services has the authority to award contracts:
    1. to the Lowest Responsive Bidder,
    2. on the basis of Best Value of a value of $750,000 or less,
    3. and for awards of non-competitive contracts of a value of $125,000 or less, (2014-443, 2017-362, 2025-33, 2025-48)
  7. The City Manager has the authority to instruct Directors not to award contracts and to submit recommendations to Council for approval and may provide additional restrictions concerning procurement where such action is considered necessary and in the best interest of the City.
  8. Where authority is delegated to a specific staff member in this by-law, the authority may be further delegated to staff members within the applicable department as outlined in the City‚Äôs organizational chart, or to Supply Services, provided that such delegation is authorized in writing and does not exceed the authority delegated by this by-law to the authorized person. Sub-delegation of authority will be limited to two reporting levels below the authorized person and cannot be further delegated. (2017-362)
  9. Delegations of authority under this Section remain valid until revoked or amended, even if the author of the delegation is no longer in office. (2023-4)

Section 6 - Requirement for approved funds

  1. The exercise of authority to award a contract is subject to the identification and availability of sufficient funds in appropriate accounts within Council approved estimates.
  2. Where goods and services are routinely purchased or leased on a multiyear basis, the exercise of authority to award a contract is subject to:
    1. the identification and availability of sufficient funds in appropriate accounts for the current year within Council approved estimates,
    2. the requirement for the goods or services will continue to exist in subsequent years and, in the opinion of the Chief Financial Officer, the required funding can reasonably be expected to be made available, (2004-565, 2021-26) and
    3. the contract has a provision in it that the supply of goods or services in subsequent years is subject to the approval by Council of the branch estimates to meet the proposed expenditures.
  3. Supply Services shall reject all purchase requests for contracts for which sufficient funds are not available and identified unless, in the opinion of Supply Services, the deficiency is minimal and alternative funding has been identified. 

Section 7 - Restrictions

  1. No requirement may be divided into two or more parts to avoid the application of the provisions of this by-law.
  2. A Director shall not award a contract where Supply Services has determined that the provisions of this by-law have not been adhered to and has so advised the Director.
  3. Supply Services shall,
    1. reject all purchase requisitions for services where the services could result in the establishment of an employee - employer relationship,
    2. with the Director, determine whether the requirement referred to in Clause 7(3)(a) can be revised, or the requirement should be forwarded to Human Resources Services for action.  (2010-291, 2017-362)

Section 8 - Total project cost

  1. Where this by-law prescribes financial limits on contracts that may be awarded on the authority of a Director, or provides for financial limits on contracts required to be reported to Council, for the purpose of determining whether a contract falls within these prescribed limits, the contract amount shall be the sum of all costs to be paid to the supplier under the contract, excluding taxes. (2021-26)

Section 9 - Prescribed council approval

  1. Despite any other provision of this by-law, the following contracts are subject to Council approval:
    1. any contract requiring approval from the Ontario Municipal Board,
    2. any contract prescribed by Statute to be made by Council,
    3. where the cost amount proposed for acceptance is higher than the Council approved branch estimates and the necessary adjustments cannot be made,
    4. where the revenue amount proposed for acceptance is lower than the Council approved branch estimates,
    5. where a major irregularity precludes the award of a tender to the supplier submitting the lowest responsive bid, (2010-291)
    6. where authority to approve has not been expressly delegated, (2010-291) and,
    7. where a contract is a result of a public private partnership (‚ÄúP3‚Äù) opportunity. (2010-291)
  2. No provision of the by-law precludes a Director or Supply Services from submitting an award to Council where:
    1. in the opinion of a Director, it is in the best interest of the City to do so, or
    2. it is a matter of procurement procedure and, in the opinion of Supply Services; it is in the best interest of the City to do so.

Section 10 - Trade agreements

  1. Procurement by the City may be subject to the provisions of trade agreements.
  2. Where an applicable trade agreement is in conflict with this by-law, the trade agreement shall take precedence.
  3. Supply Services shall advise Directors where a procurement action may not conform to an applicable trade agreement as early as possible in the procurement process. 

Section 11 - Notification of procurement opportunities

  1. Notification of procurement opportunities exceeding $125,000 for goods, services and construction shall be made by open electronic tendering means. (2006-276, 2025-33)
  2. Notification of procurement opportunities by means of open electronic tendering may be complemented by other means where appropriate.
  3. Notifications of procurement opportunities below the thresholds stated in subsection 11(1) may be made by means of open electronic tendering.
  4. If means other than electronic tendering is used for notification of procurement opportunities, consideration shall be given to ensuring wide dissemination and equal opportunity for suppliers.
  5. Source lists may be maintained on an exception basis for specific goods, services and construction if open electronic tendering or other notification means will not notify the specific supplier community of the opportunity.

Section 12 - Procurement documentation

  1. In order to maintain consistency, Supply Services shall provide instructions to Directors on procurement policies and procedures and on the structure, format and general content of procurement documentation.
  2. Supply Services shall review proposed procurement documentation to ensure clarity, reasonableness and quality and shall advise the Director of suggested improvements.
  3. Procurement documentation shall avoid use of specific products or brand names.
  4. The use of standards in procurement documentation that have been certified, evaluated, qualified, registered or verified by independent nationally recognized and industry-supported organizations such as the Standards Council of Canada shall be preferred.
  5. Notwithstanding Subsection 12(3), a Director may specify a specific product or brand name for essential functionality purposes to avoid unacceptable risk or for some other valid purpose.  In such instances, the Director and Supply Services shall manage the procurement to achieve a competitive situation if possible.
  6. Directors shall:
    1. give consideration to the need for value analysis comparisons of options or choices, and
    2. ensure that adequate value analyses comparisons are conducted to provide assurance that the specification will provide best value. (2020-144)
  7. Supply Services shall issue and maintain guidelines for the documentation and content of terms of reference and general scopes of work.
  8. The City Solicitor or the Chief Procurement Officer shall review all proposed changes to standard clauses in bid solicitations and contracts to determine if they are significant, in which case they shall review and, provided the Director is in agreement with the changes, initial as ‚ÄúApproved for Execution‚Äù prior to finalization. For bid solicitations and contracts not deemed significant, the Director may elect to proceed with finalization without a comprehensive legal review. (2004-565, 2021-26, 2025-33)

Section 13 - Legal services

  1. The procurement of legal services is exempt from a competitive process and from the use of Purchase Orders, if made in accordance with the delegation of authority to the City Solicitor. (2004-565, 2021-26)
  2. The City Solicitor shall annually report to Council on the use of external legal services in conjunction with the reporting requirement prescribed in subsection 39(2). (2006-276, 2021-26)

Section 14 - Cancellation of a bid solicitation

  1. A Director, with the concurrence of Supply Services, may request Supply Services to cancel a bid solicitation at any time up to contract award.
  2. The Director and Supply Services shall ensure that the confidentiality of any bid submitted is maintained in accordance with the provisions of the Municipal Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act, as amended.

  11. Supply Services shall maintain a list of suggested evaluation criteria for assistance in formulating an evaluation scheme using a Request for Proposal that includes factors such as qualifications and experience, strategy, approach, methodology, scheduling, past performance, community benefits, sustainability, facilities and equipment. (2025-33)
  12. Directors shall identify appropriate criteria from the list maintained by Supply Services for use in a Request for Proposal but are not limited to criteria from the list.
  13. Where a requirement has corporate-wide application or applies to two or more Departments, one Director shall, (2010-291)
    1. manage the procurement keeping other effected Directors informed, and
    2. be accountable for the authorization of the procurement.

Section 17 - Purchases not exceeding $25,000 (2014-443, 2025-33)

  1. The procedure used to purchase goods, services, or construction not exceeding $25,000 in value shall demonstrate that fair market value was achieved. (2025-33)
  2. The procedure used to make purchases exceeding $4,000 in value shall include evidence that the Director obtained a minimum of three verbal or written quotes unless subsection 17(3) applies. (2025-33)
  3. The Director may directly select a supplier to provide professional services without obtaining quotes where the total cost of the professional services does not exceed $25,000 in value. (2025-33)
  4. For purchases not exceeding $25,000 in value, the Director may delegate his or her authority to a designate provided the designate follows the requirements of this by-law. (2025-33)
  5. Purchases of goods, services, and construction not exceeding $25,000 in value incurred in the general administration of a Department for which the requirements of subsections (1) to (4) have been met may be made using:
    1. a properly authorized procurement card;
    2. a properly authorized Departmental purchase order; or
    3. a properly authorized petty cash or imprest fund. (2014-443, 2025-33)
  6. For purchases not exceeding $25,000 in value, the Director may waive the requirement for competitive bid solicitation and enter into direct negotiations under the circumstances described in 22(1) or 23, provided that:
    1. the funds are available in appropriate accounts within the Departmental estimates approved by Council including authorized revisions to meet the proposed expenditure,
    2. the supplier in whom the Director has the greatest confidence to fulfill the requirement and provides for fair market value shall be selected,
    3. the compelling rationale that warrants a non-competitive selection for approval is documented, and
    4. the provisions of Section 31 regarding the use of an agreement or Purchase Order are followed. (2025-33)

Section 18 - Purchases Not Exceeding $125,000 (2006-276, 2014-443, 2017-363, 2025-33) 

  1. Requirements estimated at $125,000 or less, shall be handled by the Request for Quotation procedure, however there may be requirements estimated at $125,000 or less where it will be more appropriate to solicit bids using a Request for Tender or a Request for Proposal. (2014-443, 2025-33)
  2. The Request for Quotation is a bid solicitation where written quotes are obtained from suppliers without formal advertising or receipt of sealed bids.
  3. The Director shall furnish to Supply Services a purchase requisition or written request containing the relevant specifications, terms and conditions for the purchase of goods, services or construction.
  4. Supply Services shall:
    1. review the specifications, terms and conditions relevant to the acquisition of goods, services or construction,
    2. suggest changes or provide other information to the Director as appropriate,
    3. conduct a Request for Quotation (2025-33):
      1. by using the electronic tendering system,
      2. by requesting submissions from those suppliers contained in a list of vendors maintained by Supply who appear best qualified to meet the provisions of the quotation,
      3. by validating quotes solicited by the Department in accordance with 17(2) (2025-33) or
      4. by any combination thereof which will result in appropriate fair market value assessment of the Request for Quotation, (2025-33)
    4. review the bids to ensure compliance with the related procedures and established terms or conditions, and
    5. consider any irregularities and act in accordance with Section 30 of this by-law.
  5. Directors or Supply Services on behalf of the concerned Director may award contracts emanating from a Request for Quotation not exceeding $125,000 provided that (2025-33):
    1. sufficient funds are available and identified in appropriate accounts within Council approved departmental estimates including authorized revisions,
    2. the award is to the lowest responsive bidder,
    3. Supply Services is in receipt of a funded requisition, and
    4. the provisions of this by-law are followed. (2014-443)
  6. Where the authority referred to in Subsection 18(5) is exercised, written documentation respecting the award of the contract is to be kept on the procurement file.
  7. The purchase of goods, services or construction referred in Subsection 18(1) shall be made through the issue of a Purchase Order by Supply Services. (2006-276)

Section 19 - Purchases Exceeding $125,000 ‚Äì Request for Tender (2006-276, 2014-443, 2017-362, 2025-33) 

  1. A Request for Tender shall be used for purchases exceeding $125,000 where all of the following criteria apply (2006-276, 2014-443, 2025-33):
    1. two or more sources are considered capable of supplying the requirement,
    2. the requirement is adequately defined to permit the evaluation of tenders against clearly stated criteria,
    3. the market conditions are such that tenders can be submitted on a common pricing basis, and
    4. it is intended to accept the lowest priced responsive tender without negotiations.
  2. The Director shall provide to Supply Services a purchase requisition containing the relevant specifications, terms and conditions for the acquisition of goods, services and construction.
  3. For road and sewer construction contracts, the Director shall establish the specifications and be responsible for the contents of the Request for Tender.

  4. Supply Services shall:

    1. review the specifications, terms and conditions relevant to the acquisition of goods, services or construction,
    2. suggest changes or provide other information to the Director as appropriate,
    3. conduct a Request for Tender:
      1. by using the electronic tendering system,
      2. requesting submissions from those suppliers contained in a list of vendors maintained by Supply who appear best qualified to meet the provisions of the tender, or
      3. by any combination thereof which will result in appropriate fair market value assessment of the Request for Tender,
    4. review the bids to ensure compliance with the related procedures and established terms or conditions, and
    5. consider any irregularities and act in accordance with Section 30 of this by-law. 

    Sealed tenders with a public opening shall be required. (2023-004, 2025-33)

  5. Supply Services shall forward to the appropriate Director a summary of the procurement and recommend the award of contract to the lowest responsive bidder. (2017-362)
  6. Directors or Supply Services on behalf of the concerned Director may award contracts emanating from a Request for Tender provided that (2014-443, 2017-362):
    1. the award is to the lowest responsive bidder,
    2. sufficient funds are available and identified in appropriate accounts within Council approved Departmental estimates including authorized revisions, (2014-443)
    3. Supply Services is in receipt of a funded requisition, and
    4. the provisions of this by-law are followed.
  7. Where the authority referred to in subsection 19(6) is exercised, written notification respecting the award of the contract is to be kept on the procurement file.
  8. The Director shall follow the provisions of Section 31 regarding the award of contract using an agreement or Purchase Order.
  9. The use of a standing offer, as defined in Section 21, shall also be an acceptable procurement process, where appropriate, for purchases exceeding $125,000. (2014-443, 2025-33)

Section 21 - Standing offer purchases

  1. A Request for Standing Offer may be used where:
    1. one or more clients repetitively order the same goods or services and the actual demand is not known in advance, or

  3. To establish prices and select sources, Supply Services shall employ the provisions contained in this by-law for the acquisition of goods, services and construction.

Section 22 - Non-competitive purchases

Section 28 - No acceptable bid or equal bids received

  1. Where bids are received in response to a bid solicitation but exceed budget, are not responsive to the requirement or do not represent fair market value, a revised solicitation shall be issued in an effort to obtain an acceptable bid unless subsection 28 (2) applies.
  2. The Director and Supply Services jointly may waive the need for a revised bid solicitation and enter negotiations with the lowest responsive bidder or the highest responsive bidder for a revenue-driven bid selection emanating from a bid solicitation under the following circumstances:
    1. the total cost of the lowest responsive bid is in excess of the funds appropriated by Council for the project or the Departmental highest responsive bid revenue is less than that made in appropriate accounts in the Council approved Departmental estimates, (2014- 443) and
    2. the Director and Supply Services agree that the changes required to achieve an acceptable bid will not change the general nature of the requirement described in the bid solicitation.
  3. The method of negotiation shall be those accepted as standard negotiating procedures that employ ethical practices.
  4. In the case of building construction contracts, where the total cost of the lowest responsive bid is in excess of the appropriation made by Council, negotiations shall be made in accordance with the guidelines established by the Canadian Construction Documents Committee.
  5. If two equal bids are received, a means of breaking the tie consistent with the provisions of the solicitation shall be employed.
  6. Factors to be considered in breaking the tie include:
    1. prompt payment discount,
    2. when delivery is an important factor, the bidder offering the best delivery date be given preference,
    3. a bidder in a position to better after sales service, with a good record in this regard, be given preference,
    4. a bidder with an overall satisfactory performance record be given preference over a bidder known to have an unsatisfactory performance record,
    5. a local bidder be given preference over a non-local bidder. (2010-291)

Section 46 - Comprehensive complaints process

  1. The Chief Procurement Officer and/or the City's contact person identified in a procurement process will be the only official points of contact for a complaint.
  2. Bidders shall not contact either elected representatives or other staff regarding the complaint when the comprehensive complaints process is engaged.
  3. The City‚Äôs Comprehensive Complaints Process consists of four phases as follows:
    1. Phase One: Mandatory Bidder Debriefing:
      1. Within five business days from the date upon which a bidder is informed that they have not been selected as the winning bidder, an unsuccessful bidder has the right to submit a written request for a formal debriefing from the contract authority responsible for the procurement at issue.
      2. The City commits to providing a formal debriefing within five business days of having received such a request.
      3. The purpose of the mandatory debriefing is to provide an unsuccessful bidder with an opportunity to learn why their bid was not selected for contract award.
      4. If the bidder remains dissatisfied after the debriefing, the bidder has an additional ten business days to prepare and file a formal complaint.
      5. The City will not consider a formal complaint from an unsuccessful bidder if they have not first sought and attended a bidder debriefing.
    2. Phase Two: Preliminary Screening:
      1. A formal complaint must be made in writing and addressed to the Chief Procurement Officer.
      2. The complaint should contain the identity of the complainant and the procurement process at issue as well as a clear and detailed statement of the legal and/or factual grounds upon which the complaint is based.
      3. The complaint should also include, as attachments, any information or documents relevant to the complaint that are in the bidder‚Äôs possession.
      4. The formal complaint is an unsuccessful bidder‚Äôs sole opportunity to present their case, and should therefore be drafted accordingly.
      5. Within ten business days of having received a formal complaint, the Chief Procurement Officer will determine whether or not it discloses a reasonable indication that the procurement was not carried out in accordance with the City‚Äôs procurement policies and/or the terms of the procurement process. (2014-443)
      6. Unless it will undermine the City‚Äôs best interests, final approval and the awarding of the contract shall be postponed pending the outcome of the complaint process. This suspension may be set aside, and final approval for the awarding of a contract may be given, at any time in the complaints process if it is in the City‚Äôs best interests to do so.
      7. If the Chief Procurement Officer concludes that the complaint does not disclose credible information to indicate that a breach of the terms of the procurement process or City policies has occurred, no further review of the complaint will take place and the contract shall be awarded in accordance with this By-law.
      8. If the complaint is found to disclose a reasonable indication that the procurement was not carried out in accordance with the City‚Äôs policies or the terms of the procurement process, the Chief Procurement Officer will take such necessary action to bring the solicitation back into compliance, which may include the cancellation of the solicitation, or the suspension or cancellation of the bid award. If the issue cannot be resolved appropriately, the Chief Procurement Officer will then refer the matter to a Review Panel, as provided in clause (c), ‚ÄúPhase Three‚Äù. (2014-443).
    3. Phase Three: Formal Review:
      1. Where the Chief Procurement Officer has received a complaint that discloses a reasonable indication that the procurement at issue was not carried out in accordance with the City‚Äôs procurement policies and/or the terms of the procurement process, it is to be considered by a Review Panel comprised of the Chief Procurement Officer, the City Solicitor (or a designated lawyer from the Legal Services), a representative from the Auditor General‚Äôs office and the Fairness Commissioner if applicable. (2021-26)
      2. The Review Panel after considering the complaint will provide a report to the Finance and Corporate Services Committee setting out its recommendation for how the complaint should be dealt with. (2014-443, 2025-33)
      3. The Review Panel report to the Committee will include a description of the procurement process at issue and an overview of the complaint received from the unsuccessful bidder. The panel report will recommend a specific course of action including, where appropriate, an explanation for why that course of action is recommended in response to the complaint.
      4. The report is to be submitted to the Committee within twenty business days from the date upon which the Chief Procurement Officer referred the complaint to the Review Panel.
    4. Phase Four: Role of Finance and Corporate Services Committee (2025-33):
      1. The Finance and Corporate Services Committee is to consider the report at the next regularly scheduled meeting. (2014-443, 2025-33)
      2. The Committee will hear any delegations at the meeting in the standard manner, including the unsuccessful bidder who filed the formal complaint.
      3. The Committee will either endorse the course of action recommended by the Review Panel or adopt an alternative course of action that it considers      appropriate under the circumstances and is consistent with the terms of the solicitation and/or the City‚Äôs procurement policies.  The Committee‚Äôs decision is final and it will not conduct a second separate review of the complaint.  (2010-291)

Section 47 - Litigation exclusion provision

    1. any other related contract or services; or
    2. any matter arising from the City‚Äôs exercise of its powers, duties, or functions. (2014-443, 2017-362, 2021-26)
  2. In determining whether or not to reject a quotation, tender or proposal under this clause, the Chief Procurement Officer and the City Solicitor will consider; (2017-362, 2021-26)
    1. whether the litigation is likely to adversely affect the bidder‚Äôs ability to work with the City, its consultants and representatives; or,
    2. whether the City‚Äôs experience with the bidder indicates that the City is likely to incur increased staff and legal costs in the administration of the contract if it is awarded to the bidder; or,
    3. whether the bidder has been convicted of a criminal act against the City or one of its local boards or corporations; or,
    4. whether the bidder has failed to satisfy an outstanding debt to the City or one of its local boards or corporations; or,
