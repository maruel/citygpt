A by-law of the City of
Ottawa to provide for solid waste management and to repeal by-law 2012-370
.
WHEREAS Sections 8, 9 and 10 of the Municipal Act, 2001, authorize a municipality to pass by-laws that are necessary or desirable for municipal purposes  and, in particular, respecting economic, social and environmental well-being of the municipality including climate change; health, safety and wellbeing of persons; services and things that the municipality is authorized to provide; and protection of persons and property;
AND WHEREAS Section 391 of the Municipal Act, 2001 provides that a municipality may pass by-laws imposing fees or charges on persons for services or activities provided or done by or on behalf of it;
NOW THEREFORE the Council of the City of Ottawa enacts as follows:
In this by-law:

#### Section 2 - Interpretation

1. This by-law includes Schedules “A”, “B”, “C”, “D”, “E”, “F”, “G”, “H”, “I”, and “J”, and these Schedules are hereby declared to form part of this by-law. Any reference herein to any statute, regulation, by-law or other law shall be construed as a reference thereto as amended or re-enacted from time to time or as a reference to any successor thereto.
1. In this by-law, the necessary grammatical changes required to make the provisions in this by-law apply to corporations, partnerships, trusts, and individuals of any gender, and include the singular or plural meaning where the context so requires, shall in all cases be assumed as fully expressed.
1. The headings inserted in the by-law are for convenience of reference only and shall not affect the construction or interpretation of this by-law.
1. If a court of competent jurisdiction declares any part, section, subsection or provision of this by-law to be invalid, illegal, unenforceable or of no force and effect, any such part, section, subsection or provision shall be deemed severable, and the remainder of this by-law shall continue in force and be applied and enforced in accordance with its terms to the fullest extent possible according to law.
1. This By-law shall not be interpreted as exempting any person from the requirement to comply with any other City by-law. In the event of conflict between the provisions of this by-law and any other City by-law, the provisions which are more protective of the environment, and the health, safety and welfare of the general public shall apply

1. This by-law applies to the collection and/or disposal of waste materials, including garbage, bulky items, special consideration items, household organic material, recyclable material, and leaf and yard waste material, from all eligible serviced locations within the geographic boundaries of the City of Ottawa, including residential, City designated IC&I properties and City facilities, and public spaces as outlined in this by-law.
1. This by-law further applies to the Trail Road Waste Facility Landfill owned and operated by the City and accessed by residents for the purpose of waste disposal and processing as outlined in this by-law.

1. The General Manager shall supervise and administer a collection system for garbage, bulky items, special consideration items, recyclable material, household organic material, leaf and yard waste material and Christmas trees and shall be responsible for its operation.
1. The General Manager shall be responsible for maintenance and operations of the Trail Waste Facility Landfill in conformity with the Environmental Protection Act and any regulations passed thereunder, and any other applicable federal or provincial laws.
1. The General Manager shall establish waste diversion programs such as the Take It Back Product Program and other programs as deemed appropriate by the City, or as required by federal or provincial legislation.
1. The General Manager shall administer and enforce this by-law with the authority to determine:
  1. the level and type of collection service provided to eligible serviced locations, including residential, multi-residential and City designated IC&I properties;
  1. the type of properties that shall be eligible for any municipal waste collection services administered through Solid Waste Services;
  1. the location where all collectable waste materials are placed for collection at eligible serviced locations, including residential, multi-residential and City designated IC&I properties;
  1. the type of approved container, potentially unique to the property, used for the collection of all collectable waste materials from eligible serviced locations, including residential, multi-residential and City designated IC&I properties;
  1. the scheduling of municipal waste collection services administered by Solid Waste Services, including collection day or changes to collection schedules or services;
  1. whether a serviced location, point of collection or property is safe for entry by any employees of the City or its contractor having regard to the physical condition and layout, loading facilities, method of handling collectible material at the point of collection;
  1. the designation of materials as the applicable types of waste materials addressed in this by-law;
  1. whether to continue or discontinue municipal waste collection services, for any waste stream and for any property serviced though the City’s Solid Waste Services, as necessary in the circumstances
  1. whether reinstatement of municipal waste collection services or access to the Trail Waste Facility Landfill is appropriate in the circumstances, in whole or in part, after having been suspended, discontinued or revoked;
  1. any limits for, and any requirements associated with, waste materials to be collected or accepted;
  1. through inspection, the contents of any container/item set out for collection by any person or owner provided service under municipal collection services;
  1. whether reinstatement of municipal waste collection service is appropriate in the circumstances, upon the owner demonstrating to the satisfaction of the General Manager that the owner will be in compliance with the provisions of this by- law;
  1. operational requirements and access rights to the Trail Waste Facility Landfill including: hours of operation, speed limits, standard operating procedures, and the types of waste accepted;
  1. Whether amendments, replacements or deletions to the Schedules under this by-law are necessary and may, from time-to-time, proceed directly to Council to amend, replace, or delete said Schedules by way of placing a by-law directly on the Agenda of Council for enactment; and
  1. any other matter necessary for the administration of this by-law or necessary for the collection, disposal and processing of waste.
1. Should emergency conditions arise that, in the opinion of the General Manager, imperil municipal or private landfill sites, recycling, or organic facilities, the General Manager shall:a. take all remedial measures as may be necessary to protect public health, which may include limiting or stopping the collection of any waste material stream in any area and restricting collection for any specific purpose;
b. expend money and employ workers as needed to restore the City’s solid waste system; and
c.
report to Council as soon as practical after such measures have been taken.
1. The Chief Financial Officer / Treasurer shall perform the following duties pursuant to the Municipal Act, 2001:a.
invoice and collect the solid waste fees or charges related to waste diversion, collection and disposal as outlined in Schedule “A”; and
b. implement enforcement provisions for outstanding fees or charges.
1. No action or other proceeding for damages shall be instituted against the General Manager, or any city employee, or city contracted employee for any act done in good faith, in execution of any power or duty under this bylaw.
1. The City may enter into a contract with any person for the performance of the whole of any part of the work described in this by-law.

#### Section 5 - Eligibility for curbside collection services

1. Owners of residential properties as defined in this by-law, are eligible to receive municipal curbside waste collection services in accordance with service levels outlined in Schedule “B”, as approved by the City.
1. Owners of IC&I properties shall be eligible for curbside waste collection services through the City’s Yellow Bag for Small Businesses Program, provided the following conditions are met, as approved by the City:
  1. eligible property shall be assessed as an IC&I property through MPAC;
  1. sum total of waste generated per location shall not exceed the set-out limits applicable to the Yellow Bag Collection Program for Small Businesses outlined in Schedule “D”;
  1. garbage shall be set out in specific City approved yellow garbage bags purchased through City approved distribution locations, at the owner’s expense at prices outlined in Schedule “A”, as amended annually; and
  1. eligible property owner shall not be receiving any private waste collection services for any collectable waste materials, as deemed by the City and outlined in this by-law, while registered and participating in the program.
1. Municipal facilities that do not exceed the waste set-out limits shall be eligible to receive curbside waste collection services, as outlined in Schedule “B”.
1. Every owner of an eligible serviced location shall comply with all provisions of this by-law in order to receive curbside waste collection services.
1. Schools registered through the City’s Green Bin in Schools Pilot, as designated by the General Manager, shall receive curbside waste collection of household organic material as outlined in Schedule “B”, provided the material is set out in a City approved container as outlined in Schedule “D”.

#### Section 6 - Fees for curbside collection services

1. Owners of a residential property eligible for curbside waste collection services, as approved by the General Manager, shall pay the applicable City of Ottawa Solid Waste Curbside Collection User Fee as outlined in Schedule “A”, as amended annually.
1. All fees and charges levied under this Section and which are in arrears shall be subject to a monthly interest charge.
1. Owners of residential properties eligible for curbside collection services setting out garbage above the set-out limit outlined in Schedule “B” shall set-out garbage in a City approved yellow bag to be purchased at the owner’s expense, as outlined in Schedule “A”.
1. Owners of eligible IC&I properties registered in the Yellow Bag Program for Small Businesses shall set out garbage in a City approved yellow bag to be purchased at the owner’s expense, as outlined Schedule “A”.

1. The City shall provide curbside collection of garbage, bulky items, special considerations items, recyclable material, household organic material, leaf and yard waste material and Christmas trees to all eligible properties approved for curbside collection services in accordance with Schedule “B”.
1. Garbage and bulky items shall be collected curbside on a bi-weekly basis per scheduled collection day from eligible serviced locations in accordance with theprovisions of this by-law and as outlined in Schedule “B”, except when the scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday as provided herein.
1. Special consideration items shall be collected curbside on a bi-weekly basis per scheduled collection day from eligible serviced locations, registered with the special consideration program, in accordance with the provisions of this by-law and as outlined in Schedule “B”, except when the scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday as provided herein. The collection of special consideration items will occur on the opposite week of regular bi-weekly garbage and bulky item collection.
1. Household organic material shall be collected curbside on a weekly basis, per scheduled collection day, from eligible serviced locations, in accordance with the provisions of this by-law and as outlined in Schedule “B”, except when the scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday as provided herein.
1. Leaf and yard waste material shall be collected curbside on a weekly basis, per scheduled collection day, from eligible serviced locations, in accordance with the provisions of this by-law and as outlined in Schedule “B”, except when the scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday as provided herein.
1. Recyclable material shall be collected on a weekly basis per scheduled collection day, from eligible serviced locations, based on alternating weekly set-outs of (1) paper fibre materials (Black Box) and, (2) glass, metal and plastic materials (Blue Box), in accordance with the provisions in this by-law and as outlined in Schedule “B”, except when the scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday as provided herein.
1. No curbside collection shall be made on the following Statutory Holidays which fall on scheduled collection days: New Year's Day, Family Day, Good Friday, Easter Monday, Victoria Day, Canada Day, Civic Holiday, Labour Day, Thanksgiving Day or Christmas Day, unless an emergency or exceptional situation, as deemed by the City, arises and collection must occur on a holiday.
1. When a scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday, the collection shall be made one (1) day later for the remainder of the week, or as approved by the General Manager.
1. Curbside collection services shall occur between 7:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. Monday to Friday in accordance with this by-law, except when the scheduled collection day falls on a Statutory Holiday, as approved by the General Manager.
1. No curbside collection services shall be made on a Sunday unless an emergency or exceptional situation, as approved by the General Manager, arises and collection must occur on a Sunday.

1. No owner shall exceed the set-out limits outlined in Schedule “B”, unless otherwise exempted through this by-law.
1. Owners of agricultural properties with a residential property on-site eligible for curbside collection services shall be exempt from the set-out limit applicable to garbage, as outlined in Schedule “B”, from April 1 to May 31 every year.
  1. Notwithstanding Subsection 8(2), owners of agricultural properties with a residential property on-site eligible for curbside collection services shall be exempt from the garbage set-out limit, as outlined in Schedule “B”, for applicable farm waste and agricultural waste only.
    1. Paragraph (a) shall be deemed to be repealed effective January 1, 2026, unless otherwise extended by Council.
1. No owner shall fail to properly dispose of non-collectable waste, as identified in Schedule “E”, or to arrange for disposal of such waste at the sole expense of the owner. The disposal of non-collectable waste shall be in compliance with all applicable federal, provincial and municipal laws as they may relate to the waste described in Schedule “E”.
1. The City’s curbside waste collection service shall not include the collection and removal of any non-collectable waste as outlined in Schedule “E”, unless approved by the General Manager.
1. The City shall not collect waste of any kind from any residential property until a final occupancy permit is issued for said property.
1. The City shall not collect waste of any kind from unoccupied residential properties.

1. No owner shall set out any collectable waste, at the point of collection earlier than 6:00 p.m. on the previous evening of the scheduled collection day and later than 7:00 a.m. on the scheduled collection day.
1. No owner shall fail to ensure that all collectable waste is set out at the point of collection no later than 7:00 a.m. on the scheduled collection day in order to receive collection services.
1. No owner shall place or permit the set out of non-collectable waste, as outlined in Schedule “E”, at the point of collection unless approved by the General Manager.
1. No owner shall fail to set out collectable waste in the appropriate approved container, as outlined in Schedule “D”, for curbside waste collection services.
1. No owner shall set out a container for collection that, as deemed by the City:
  1. is not an approved container in size and volume;
  1. emits a foul or offensive odour;
  1. harbours or attracts rats, other vermin or insects;
  1. contains waste that falls out of or protrudes from it;
  1. is unclean or unsanitary;
  1. has graffiti; or
  1. is damaged to the extent that it is unsafe to manoeuvre.
1. No owner shall fail to maintain, repair or replace an approved container, as outlined in Schedule “D”, at the owner’s expense, unless it has been damaged through collection operations, as deemed by the City.
1. No owner shall fail to remove or return any empty containers to the owner’s private property before 10:00 p.m. on the scheduled collection day.

In the recovery or enforcement of penalties and forfeitures incurred, or in any other proceeding in relation to matters that have happened before the repeal of By-law No. 2012-370, the Solid Waste Management By-law, the procedure established by this by-law shall be followed so far as it can be adopted.

1. By-law 2012-370, and any amendments there to, are repealed as of the in-force date of this by-law.

1. This by-law may be referred to as the Solid Waste Services By-law.

1. This by-law shall come into force and take effect on December 2, 2024.

### Waste Collection Fees

1. The annual fees for residential garbage collection, levied on the municipal tax bill, are payable at the time of payment of municipal taxes as follows:
  1. $243.00 per residential dwelling unit of each separate municipal tax assessed property receiving residential curbside collection service; and
  1. $167.00 per residential dwelling unit of each separate municipal tax assessed property receiving multi-residential containerized collection service.

In the case of payments received by mail, the date the payment is received by the City shall be taken as the day of payment.
The fee per yellow garbage bag (residential and small business), payable at the time of purchase from a City retailer, for waste to be collected through the Yellow Bag program is $4.40 per bag, sold in quantities of 4.

Non-Collectable Waste Includes:
1. Any waste materials not set out in accordance with the provisions of this by-law;
1. Appliances
1. Automobile parts;
1. Construction and demolition waste as defined in this by-law including but not limited to: concrete, cinder blocks, paving stones, asphalt;
1. Contents of cesspools and outdoor latrines;
1. Contents of grease traps;
1. Electrical and Electronic Equipment as defined in Schedule 1 of O. Reg 522/20 including but not limited to: light bulbs, tubes and lamps, including incandescent, fluorescent, halogen, light emitting diode (LED) and high intensity discharge (HID) lamps (excluding string and/or patio lights)
1. Hay, straw, farm animal manure or excrement;
1. Hazardous waste as defined in O. Reg 347 including but not limited to:
  1. Acids and caustic materials such as pool chemicals, fire extinguishers
  1. Aerosol containers (full or partially full)
  1. Batteries
  1. Engine oil, brake, transmission fluid;
  1. Explosives or highly combustible materials such as gasoline, motor oil, barbeque or lighter fluid, ammunition, weapons, or other flammable material;
  1. Household cleaners including bleach, oven, drain, toilet and carpet cleaning solutions;
  1. Leachate or toxic waste such as septic tank pumpings, raw sewage and sludge;
  1. Paints, solvents, coatings and wood preservatives;
  1. Poisons, fertilizers, fungicides, herbicides, insecticides or pesticides;
  1. Pressurized containers including propane cylinders and tanks;
  1. Radioactive material;
1. Household hazardous waste materials as defined in the terms in this by-law
1. IC& I waste not approved for collection through the City’s Yellow Bag Program for Small Businesses.
1. Imported waste material as defined in this By-law
1. Material which may be classed as a “designated substance” pursuant to the Occupational Health and Safety Act;
1. Railway ties;
1. Soil, earth, stone, boulders, trees, tree stumps, tree trunks, firewood and tree cuttings over 7 cm in diameter;
1. Trade waste including any stock and or by-products of wholesale or retail or of any industrial or commercial process;
1. Special waste material as defined in this By-law
1. Swill, liquid waste or organic matter, which has not been drained;
1. Pathological waste as defined in O. Reg 347 including but not limited to:
  1. Needles, sharps, syringes, glass stem pipes, lancets, insulin tips;
  1. Carcasses of any animal (including animal parts), fowl or live animal with the exception of bona fide household organic material;
  1. Medications, pharmaceuticals and drugs;
  1. Hazardous medical waste
1. Tires (excluding bicycle tires);
1. Toilets, not dismantled;
1. Wooden pallets; and
1. Any other item designated as non-collectable material as approved by the General Manager and amended from time to time.

Acceptable Bulky Items Include:
1. Carpet;
1. Furniture;
1. Garden, yard and outdoor equipment such as pools or parts thereof, fence, wire, tarps, swing sets, and pool or hot tub covers;
1. Large toys;
1. Mattresses; and
1. Any other large, discarded material as approved by the General Manager at their sole discretion.

Black Box Material:
1. Newspapers and flyers;
1. Corrugated cardboard;
1. Boxboard;
1. Soft cover books/Magazines/catalogues;
1. Fine paper;
1. Advertising mail;
1. Egg cartons (paper);
1. Paper cores;
1. Kraft/paper bags;
1. Paper gift-wrap and greeting cards;
1. Other clean paper packaging; and
1. Any other recyclable material as per O. Reg. 391/21, as amended.

Blue Box Material:
1. Clear and coloured glass jars and bottles;
1. Steel/aluminum food and beverage cans;
1. Aluminum foil;
1. Gable top cartons;
1. Aseptic drinking boxes;
1. All rigid plastic food and beverage containers with recycling symbols numbered 1 through 7;
1. Metal lids;
1. Spiral-wound containers with metal ends;
1. Empty paint cans & aerosol containers; and
1. Any other packaging and recyclable material as per O. Reg. 391/21 as amended

Acceptable Household Organic Material Includes:
1. Baked goods and baking ingredients including cake, cookies, sugar, spices, eggs and eggshells;
1. Dairy products including cheese, and yogurt;
1. Fats including lard, butter, and solidified oils
1. Fruits and vegetables including peels, rinds, seeds and husks
1. Grain products including pasta, bread, cereal and rice;
1. Hair, pet hair, and feathers;
1. Leaf and yard waste material, brush, house plants and cut flowers;
1. Meat and fish products including bones, fat, skin and shells;
1. Nuts, seeds and shells;
1. Other food products including candy, coffee grounds, coffee filters and tea bags;
1. Pet waste including, household pet feces (in a sealed leak-proof bag), pet bedding and kitty litter;
1. Pumpkins;
1. Sauces, spices, dressings and spreads;
1. Small amounts of cooking oil (soaked in bread or paper towel;)
1. Small amounts of shredded paper, and sawdust (natural wood sources only);
1. Soiled and/or food soiled paper products including tissues, paper towels, cardboard egg cartons, paper coffee cups, paper plates and cups, paper take-out food containers, microwave popcorn bags, flour and sugar bags, pizza boxes, newspaper, parchment paper;
1. Wooden food products including popsicle sticks, toothpicks; and
1. Any other item designated as household organic material as approved by the General Manager and amended from time to time.

Acceptable Leaf and Yard Waste Material Includes:
1. Branches (no larger than 7 cm in diameter, and no longer than 1.2 m in length);
1. Garden plants, and flowers (soil removed);
1. Grass and grass clippings;
1. Hedge, shrub and tree trimmings;
1. Leaves, brush and vines;
1. Natural Christmas trees;
1. Non-noxious weeds;
1. Wind fallen fruit; and
1. Any other item designated as Leaf and Yard Waste Material as approved by the General Manager and amended from time to time.

Acceptable Special Consideration Items Include:
1. Casts;
1. Catheters;
1. Colostomy bags (empty and rinsed);
1. Dialysis wastes (tubing, filters, disposable towels, and sheets);
1. Diapers;
1. Disposable pads;
1. Gloves, masks, gowns, and aprons;
1. Gastric and nasal tubes (empty and rinsed);
1. Incontinence products;
1. IV bags and tubing (empty and rinsed);
1. Used dressings (not blood saturated); and
1. Any other item designated as Special Consideration Items as approved by the General Manager and amended from time to time.

Items excluded from the Special Consideration Items Program include:
1. Hazardous waste such as blood-saturated items;
1. Needles/Sharps; and
1. Sharp containers.
